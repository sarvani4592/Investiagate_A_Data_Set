def split(column):
    #will recieve a column that has strings seperated by pipe 
    data = df1[column].str.cat(sep='|')
    
    #giving input as pandas series and storing values individually in different rows
    data = pd.Series(data.split('|'))
    
    #arranging in descending order
    count = data.value_counts(ascending=False)
    
    return count

Conclusions¶

From our Analysis we discovered some patterns and trends in revenue year to year, budget year to year , (budget,runtime and revenue )vs popularity of the movies and average runtimes of the movies in general.

Most of the analysis were made by grouping the movies under production companies and their populairty were discovered based on popularity rating and revenue rating

Line plots helped us analysing the max and min values of a particular variable in popular productions.

Limitations of the dataset

This data is tentative. Many rows had missing values for budget and revenue columns.

The analysis is made only on the rows who budget and revenue columns were not zeros or null. The data for those fields might have been existed , but it is not recorded in the table. There could be many promising successful movies ,which were not considered here because of missing values.

Dropping rows with missing values might have affected the entire analysis.

Currency is not given in the dataset. It can be possible that different currency units will make the budget and revenue columns vary and the entire analysis wrong.

The analysis is strictly made on the data available after deleting missing, null columns.

There is no evidence the given data is accurate and true. Hence one cant suggest this is complete and true analysis , but can recommend these suggestions for profitable movies.

A further investigation is required on cases like revenue vs popularity to get more clarity on the trends.
